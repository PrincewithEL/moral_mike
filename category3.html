Substance Abuse and Addictions

Substance abuse involves using drugs or alcohol in ways that are harmful to our health and well-being. Addiction occurs when someone can’t stop using these substances despite knowing they’re harmful. Imagine being stuck in a loop where you constantly need something that hurts you. Substance abuse can damage your body, ruin relationships, and make achieving your goals very difficult. It's important to stay informed about the dangers of drugs and alcohol and seek help if you or someone you know is struggling with addiction. Staying drug-free helps you stay healthy and focused on your dreams.Below are some of the statistics from NACADA about the status of drugs and substance use in Kenya:



One in every 6 Kenyans aged 15 – 65 years (4,733,152) were currently using at least one drug or substance of abuse.

One in every 8 Kenyans aged 15 – 65 years (3,199,119) were currently using alcohol.

One in every 12 Kenyans aged 15 – 65 years (2,305,929) were currently using tobacco.

One in every 28 Kenyans aged 15 – 65 years (964,737) were currently using khat (Miraa).

One in every 53 Kenyans aged 15 – 65 years (518,807) were currently using cannabis.

One in every 500 Kenyans aged 15 – 65 years (60,407) were currently using prescription drugs.

One in every 15 Kenyans aged 15 – 65 years (1,766,583) were currently using multiple drugs.

The 25-35 years age group representing youth out-of-school was identified as a vulnerable group for drugs and substance use and dependence. The survey showed evidence of underage drug and substance use despite the well-documented negative implications and consequences of early initiation.


Alcohol continues to be the most widely used substance of abuse in Kenya with findings pointing towards increasing demand for cheaper and readily available alcoholic products especially chang’aa, traditional brews, and potable spirits.

The prevalence of cannabis use almost doubled over the last five years. The growing demand for cannabis especially among the youth could be attributed to the low perception of harm due to myths, misinformation, and misconceptions.

Drug and substance use was identified as a key risk factor for depressive disorders. This finding presents addiction professionals with an evolving challenge of co-occurring substance use and mental health disorders.

Although the general trend shows that the overall prevalence of drugs and substance use was on a slow downward trend, the high burden of Substance Use Disorders (SUDs) presents the greatest challenge to the next phase of the campaign.

There was evidence of the online sale of drugs and substances of abuse thereby posing serious public health safety concerns as well as regulatory challenges that include access to underage children. This may be further complicated by the penetration of mobile transfer services in Kenya and the expansion of internet access services.



Moral Degradation of Society

Recent surveys indicate that 85% of people believe a lack of morals is a major issue in society today. This growing concern is also evident in various parts of Africa, including Kenya, where ethical and moral challenges have been highlighted by several studies. Below are some statistics on the downfall of morality in soceity:

Corruption

According to Transparency International's 2022 Corruption Perceptions Index, Kenya scored 30 out of 100, indicating a high level of perceived public sector corruption. This score reflects widespread unethical behaviour in government and business.
Crime Rates

A report by the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) showed a significant increase in reported crimes, with over 77,000 criminal cases recorded in 2021, up from 69,000 in 2020. This rise in crime indicates a decline in moral values and respect for the law.
Youth Unemployment and Crime

The World Bank reported that youth unemployment in Kenya is at 17.3%. High unemployment rates often correlate with increased crime and substance abuse as young people struggle to find opportunities and turn to unlawful means for survival.
Substance Abuse

According to the National Authority for the Campaign Against Alcohol and Drug Abuse (NACADA), 12.2% of Kenyans aged 15-65 were reported to be using alcohol excessively, with 9.1% engaging in binge drinking. This highlights a growing problem of substance abuse that undermines moral and healthy lifestyles.
School Cheating Scandals

In Kenya, there have been numerous instances of exam cheating, with significant numbers of students caught using unauthorised materials during national exams. In 2019, the Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC) reported that 1,309 candidates were found cheating during the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) exams.
Domestic Violence

The Kenya Demographic and Health Survey (KDHS) 2014 reported that 45% of women aged 15-49 have experienced physical violence, and 14% have experienced sexual violence at some point in their lives. These figures indicate serious moral and ethical issues within households.
Internet and Social Media

According to a report by the Communications Authority of Kenya, there has been a significant rise in cases of cyberbullying and online fraud. In 2020, the Authority reported a 50% increase in cyber-related complaints compared to the previous year. This surge reflects the growing challenges of maintaining ethical standards in the digital age.
